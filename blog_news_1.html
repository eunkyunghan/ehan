Reflections from Ky Moma Website Project

Areas I found still unclear and worth exploring further.

Did I know accessibility?

I placed an accessibility icon as a red dot on the left side, but I realized I had limited understanding of its true meaning. I want to learn how to transform a generic platform into one that is genuinely accessible.

Mobile first design is too bad for me!

I initially chose a mobile-first approach, believing it would be easier than starting with desktop. However, I struggled to adapt the design for larger web browsers, and the progress stalled midway. This taught me the importance of planning for both contexts from the start.

Responsive Website ≠ WebApp

A responsive website may look different across mobile and desktop browsers. In contrast, a web app tends to deliver more consistent visuals across platforms, though variations can still occur depending on the browser or viewport.

Anyway, I got the certificate!

It took me one year to complete the Google UX/UI Professional Certificate, initiated through MART 371 at Canada College. I’m grateful for discovering Coursera courses through this class. I even invested in a yearly membership to continue learning and address the questions raised in these projects.
